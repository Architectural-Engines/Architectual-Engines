7 **Amendments & Revisions**

7.1 **Process for Updating Bylaws or Governance Documents**
    Amendments to the bylaws or related governance documents are tightly controlled to ensure stability, transparency, and fidelity to the mission.

7.2 **Key Rules**:

    7.2a Approval Thresholds:
    Standard amendments require a 90% supermajority of all human Board votes.
    Amendments affecting exclusion rules, surplus allocation, or mission-critical clauses require 100% unanimous approval, 
    including the Founder (or Founder’s appointed Steward on the Advisory Council) plus external auditor verification.

    7.2b Proposal Submission:
    Any director may submit a proposal as a formal GitHub pull request.
    Proposal must include rationale, affected sections, and expected impacts.
    Proposals automatically timestamped via OpenTimestamps upon submission.

7.3 **Advisory Input**:
    Advisory Council may provide non-binding commentary.
    AI evaluations may be appended for reference but carry zero formal vote.

7.4 **Review & Audit Frequency**

    7.4a Scheduled Reviews:
    Full governance review occurs annually to ensure clarity, completeness, and operational alignment.
    Audit reports, including any recommended changes, must be published on GitHub within 30 days of completion.

    7.4b Ad-Hoc Reviews:
    Triggered by significant organizational events (e.g., major funding, project scale-up, legal/regulatory changes).
    Must follow the same approval and transparency rules as standard amendments.

    7.4c External Audit:
    Independent third-party auditors review both proposed and implemented amendments for compliance, accuracy, and integrity.
    Audit findings are permanently logged on GitHub and timestamped via OpenTimestamps.

7.5 **Member / Stakeholder Feedback Mechanism**
    7.5a Although Architectural Engines does not maintain a formal membership,
    all stakeholders and contributors have structured avenues to provide input on revisions:

    7.5b GitHub Feedback Submission:
    Suggestions, concerns, or proposals are submitted via issues or pull requests in the governance repository.
    Submissions automatically timestamped via OpenTimestamps.

    7.5c Public Comment Period:
    Each proposed amendment enters a 14-day public comment period.
    Comments are logged publicly, and responses from the Board or Advisory Council are required.

    7.5d Incorporation of Feedback:
    Feedback must be reviewed by all Board members before final vote.
    Rationale for including or rejecting each comment must be logged and timestamped.

7.6 **Immutable Record & Transparency**
        All proposed, reviewed, and enacted amendments are permanently recorded in the GitHub governance repository.
        Timestamp proofs via OpenTimestamps create verifiable historical integrity.
        Any failure to follow the amendment process automatically triggers public disclosure and may initiate the Automatic Fork Provision (Section Y).
