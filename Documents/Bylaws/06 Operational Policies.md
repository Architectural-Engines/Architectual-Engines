**These bylaws represent a conceptual governance framework and statement of founding intent. They have not been formally adopted, legally filed, or enacted. Official bylaws will be established upon nonprofit formation in accordance with applicable law.**

**Operational Policies**

**Code of Conduct & Ethics**
All stakeholders of Architectural Engines — including Board members, Advisory Council members, Contributors,
and Volunteers — are expected to uphold the mission and maintain the highest standards of integrity and transparency.

**Key Principles**:

**Mission fidelity**:

-Every action must support the stated mission of advancing human-aligned intelligent systems and returning 

-100% of surplus to verified public benefit projects.

**Respect and professionalism**: 

-Communication must be civil, constructive, and respectful in all internal and external interactions.

-Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation of any kind is strictly prohibited.

**No personal enrichment**:

-Stakeholders must not seek or accept personal financial gain from Anneal, its forks, 
or related projects beyond pre-approved facilitation compensation (capped at local median wage).

**Transparency**:

-All decisions, communications, and relevant outputs must be logged in the public GitHub ledger and timestamped via OpenTimestamps.

**Enforcement**:

-Violations are reviewed immediately by the Board, with a report published publicly within 48 hours 
(excluding sensitive personal data). 

-Sanctions may include removal from the Board, Advisory Council, or Contributor/Volunteer status,temporary suspension,
or mandatory corrective action.

-Repeat or severe violations automatically trigger the Automatic Fork Provision (Section Y).

**Conflict Resolution & Grievance Procedures**:

Architectural Engines maintains a structured, transparent framework for resolving disputes,
ensuring accountability and fairness for all stakeholders.

**Process Overview**:

Direct Resolution: Stakeholders are encouraged to first attempt resolution directly with the involved party.

**Conflict Review Panel**:

If unresolved, disputes escalate to a panel of three independent directors or Advisory Council members not involved in the conflict.

**Formal Grievance Submission**:

-Written grievances are submitted to the GitHub ledger.

-Sensitive personal or financial information may be included in a private, encrypted channel accessible only to the panel.

-Panel deliberates and issues a ruling within 30 days.

**Public Disclosure**:

-Decisions, reasoning, and resolution steps are posted publicly on GitHub, with personal data redacted as necessary.

**Automatic Escalation**:

Any violation that breaches bylaws or triggers the Automatic Fork Provision must be timestamped via OpenTimestamps within 48 hours and publicly disclosed.

**Additional Notes**:

-AI advisory outputs may be used as input for conflict analysis but carry zero formal voting power.

-Directors must provide a one-line justification if their decision diverges from AI advisory input.

**Intellectual Property (IP) & Data Handling**:

-All IP and data generated within Architectural Engines are handled to maximize public benefit, reproducibility, and transparency.

**Code and Contributions**:

-All contributions to Anneal, forks, or related tools are released under CC0.

-Contributors receive public attribution via GitHub commits, logs, or project acknowledgments.

-No stakeholder may claim exclusive rights or ownership.

**Data Handling**:

-Research, project, or operational data must be handled in accordance with Sections 3.3 and 8.1 (public ledger & timestamping).

-Stakeholders may not use data for personal gain.

-Sensitive data (e.g., donor info or grant applications) must be handled in secure, private channels and disclosed only when approved.

**External Collaborations**:

Any IP created in collaboration with third parties must be openly licensed (CC0) or fully disclosed to the Board and publicly posted.

Modifications to licensing, usage rights, or distribution require a 90% supermajority vote and a public ledger entry.

**Enforcement and Audit**:

-The Board ensures compliance with IP and data handling policies via annual external audits.

-Any deviation triggers public disclosure and may lead to removal or corrective action.

**Operational Escalation Flow**:

**Step 1**:Issue arises (ethics, IP, data, conflict) → logged on GitHub

**Step 2**:Internal review / direct resolution attempt

**Step 3**:Escalation to Conflict Review Panel or Board vote

**Step 4**:AI advisory may be consulted (advisory only)

**Step 5**:Decision made, timestamped via OpenTimestamps, and logged publicly

**Step 6**: Enforcement (removal, corrective action, or fork trigger)

**This structure ensures full traceability, accountability, and public verification,
while keeping the system resistant to private manipulation or hidden biases.**
