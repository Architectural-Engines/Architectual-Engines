**These bylaws represent a conceptual governance framework and statement of founding intent. They have not been formally adopted, legally filed, or enacted. Official bylaws will be established upon nonprofit formation in accordance with applicable law.**


**Membership & Stakeholders**

**Types of Stakeholders (no formal “members”)**:

**Architectural Engines has no formal membership structure. Stakeholders are defined as follows**:

-Board of Directors (3–7 individuals, self-perpetuating)

-Responsible for mission fidelity, fund allocation, and major decisions.

-Initial board appointed by founder.

-New directors added or removed by 90% supermajority vote of current board
        .
-3 years, renewable once (maximum 6 years continuous service).
        
**Advisory Council (honorary, non-voting)**:
        
-Experts, researchers, or contributors invited by the board.

-No decision power, only advisory input (technical review, funding suggestions, conceptual guidance).
        
-Can be removed by simple majority board vote.

-Founder Steward / Advisor (special Advisory Council position)

-Reserved exclusively for the original founder once stepping down from the Board of Directors.

**Role & Powers**:

**Founder**:
Non-voting, but provides mandatory advisory input on all governance or mission-critical decisions.
Can propose agenda items and review amendments affecting mission, bylaws, or surplus allocation.
Must be consulted on changes impacting founder-defined clauses.

**Protections**:
**Position cannot be removed or amended without**:
-100% unanimous board vote
-Independent external auditor verification.
-Ensures the founder cannot be voted off or bypassed immediately.

**Term**:
-Indefinite unless the founder voluntarily resigns.

**Contributors & Volunteers** (open to anyone)
Anyone who contributes code, tuning, documentation, installations, or research.
No formal rights or voting power.
Recognized publicly (GitHub credits, acknowledgments).

**Rights & Responsibilities**

**Board of Directors**:

**Rights**:

Vote on all governance, budget, and mission decisions. 90% supermajority required for changes to bylaws, mission, or surplus allocation.

**Responsibilities**: 
-Fiduciary duty to mission, annual audit compliance, transparency reporting, ensuring zero personal benefit from Anneal.

**Compensation**: 
-Capped at local median wage for facilitation work only; no salary for board service.

-Advisory Council

**Rights**:

Provide non-binding input, attend board meetings as observers.

**Responsibilities**: 

Offer expertise, maintain confidentiality on sensitive matters if requested.

**Founder Steward / Advisor**

**Rights**:

-Mandatory advisory input on governance, mission, and surplus allocation; propose agenda items; review amendments.

**Responsibilities**:
Maintain alignment with mission, review proposals affecting founder-defined clauses, provide guidance without voting.

**Contributors & Volunteers**:

**Rights**: 

-Full access to CC0 code, public attribution for contributions.

**Responsibilities**: 

-Follow CC0 license, no claim of ownership or exclusivity.

**Process for Joining, Leaving, or Removal**
**Joining Board**:

-Requires 90% supermajority vote of current board.

**Nominee must submit**:

-Statement of alignment with mission

-Conflict-of-interest disclosure

-Commitment to zero personal benefit from Anneal

-Public announcement on GitHub within 7 days.

**Leaving Board**:

-Voluntary resignation at any time (written notice to board).
    
-Automatic upon violation of bylaws (e.g., attempting private benefit, breaching confidentiality).
    
-Removal by 90% supermajority vote (documented rationale, publicly posted on GitHub).
    
**Advisory Council**:

-Invited by board vote (simple majority).

-Can resign at will.

-Removed by simple majority board vote.

**Founder Steward / Advisor**:

-Cannot be removed except by 100% unanimous board vote + independent external auditor verification.

-Position is indefinite unless founder voluntarily resigns.

**NDA / Confidentiality**:

-No blanket NDA — all governance is public (GitHub ledger).

-Limited confidentiality for sensitive personal/financial data (donor info, grant applications in progress).

-Breach of limited confidentiality = automatic removal + public disclosure.

**Funding & Surplus (cross-reference)**:

-All surplus after research budget goes 100% to verified humanitarian causes.

-No board member or stakeholder receives more than median wage for facilitation work.

-Founder receives zero compensation beyond travel reimbursement.
