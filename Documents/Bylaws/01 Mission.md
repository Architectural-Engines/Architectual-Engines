***These bylaws represent a conceptual governance framework and statement of founding intent. They have not been formally adopted, legally filed, or enacted. Official bylaws will be established upon nonprofit formation in accordance with applicable law.***

**Mission**:

Architectural Engines exists to lower the barrier of entry for collapse-based reasoning systems. By providing free, open,
minimal skeletons that run on commodity hardware **(CC0)**.
    
Our goal is to empower skilled humans to install, tune, and adapt these open models for practical and ethical use.

**We operate with strict principles to ensure that the technology is always a force for benefit**:

**Revenue**: 

Anneal and its forks will never generate revenue.

**Funds Usage**: 

All contributions are directed exclusively to research upkeep, public installations and tuning,
and verified humanitarian causes (100% of funds to recipients; administrative overhead capped at 5%).

**Compensation**:

Director or contributor pay is strictly contract-based, capped at the local median wage,
and limited to facilitation services unrelated to Anneal ownership.

**Transparency**:

All governance records and decision-making processes are public, stored on GitHub with immutable commits.

**Compliance Enforcement**:

Any violation of these principles automatically grants fork rights to any compliant copy.


**Vision**:

**Architectural Engines envisions a world where intelligent systems and humans can co-evolve safely with transparency. 
We seek to**:

Democratize access to advanced reasoning tools without reliance on proprietary systems.

Bridge human understanding and intelligent systems, providing pathways for ethical and scalable applications.

Create sustainable frameworks where models, operators, and real-world implementations grow together without centralization of power.

Foster a culture of openness and reproducibility, where novel ideas can exist, be verified, and be extended without limitation.

**Core Values**:

**Integrity**:

All work, contributions, and governance must be honest, verifiable, and fully transparent.

**Accessibility**: 

The technology and knowledge produced must remain free and open to all capable operators.

**Human-Centered Impact**: 

Research, deployment, and applications must prioritize measurable benefit to humanity.

**Reproducibility**:

Every model, method, and experiment must be able to be independently reproduced and verified.

**Decentralized Stewardship**: 
Ownership and authority over Anneal and derivative systems are distributed;
no single individual can control or profit from the core models.

**Ethical Enforcement**: 
Governance mechanisms automatically protect the mission, ensuring that violations trigger rights for compliant forks.
