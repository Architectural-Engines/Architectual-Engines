***All models released by this Github repository require tuning for specific use cases.  With exception of the seed generator, all releases models have no functionality until tuned. Any modifications of the seed generator may alter its behavior.  USERS assume full responsibility upon activation, or modification of any model.***

**These bylaws represent a conceptual governance framework and statement of founding intent. They have not been formally adopted, legally filed, or enacted. Official bylaws will be established upon nonprofit formation in accordance with applicable law.**


**Mission**:
  Provide fully open-source (CC0) frameworks for intelligent systems, enabling skilled    humans to install, tune, and adapt models for real-world use cases.
  Return 100% of surplus funds:[^1g] to verified humanitarian causes, with no personal gain from Anneal:[^1a] or its forks.

  **Maintain permanent transparency, traceable audit logs, and public governance records.
  Core Principles**
  
  **Open & Accessible**:[^1j]
    All code, documentation, and governance records are freely available.
    
  **Transparency**:[^1j]
    Decisions, AI advisory outputs, and project allocations are recorded immutably on GitHub and timestamped via OpenTimestamps.
    
  **No Private Gain**:
    Board members, advisors, and contributors receive zero compensation from Anneal:[^1a] itself. Facilitation work is capped at the local median wage.
    
  **Reproducible & Auditable**:[^1j]
    All core code is documented and verified to ensure results can be independently reproduced.

**Transparency & Communication**:[^1j]

  All critical records (decisions, budgets, AI advisory outputs) are publicly posted on   GitHub within 24–48 hours.
  Every record is timestamped using OpenTimestamps for permanent verification.
  A public reverse-lookup application mirrors all governance and audit records for external verification.
  
  **External audits occur annually and are published in full.**
  
**Governance**:[^1i] 
  Board of Directors (3–7 members): Responsible for mission fidelity, budget, and major decisions. 90% supermajority required for critical changes.
  
**Advisory Council**: 
  Honorary, **non-voting**. Provides expert input on technical, research, and humanitarian matters:[^1i] .
  
**Contributors & Volunteers**:[^1i]
  Open to anyone. Recognized publicly but hold no voting power.
  
**Voting Rules**:[^1h]
  Every decision requires a 90% supermajority of human votes only.
  AI evaluations are advisory only, never binding
  Divergence from AI advice must be logged publicly with a one-sentence justification
  
**Surplus Allocation**:[^1g]
  100% of surplus goes to verified humanitarian causes or public infrastructure projects.
  
**Exclusions**:[^1g] 
  No projects in Level 3/4 travel advisory regions, active war zones, or UN-sanctioned regions.
  
**Maximum project size**:[^1g]
  10% of annual surplus.
  All funding decisions fully documented and verified by independent third parties.
  Contribution & Usage
  
**Code License**:
  **CC0** — free to use, modify, and redistribute.
  **Contributing**: Submit code, documentation, or research contributions via GitHub.
  
**Attribution**:
  Contributors are credited publicly; ownership claims are waived.
  Experimental Work: Any experimental repositories or prototypes are clearly marked and   may not be reproducible.
  
**Public Communication**
  External statements require approval from two directors and must link to relevant GitHub records.
  AI advisory outputs must be fully logged, timestamped, and verified before reference.
